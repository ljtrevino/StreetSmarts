Energy Management

Due to the nature of our project, energy management was difficult to incorporate. Emergency devices need to constantly update in order to ensure that a person in need of help can be assisted as soon as possible. That being said, we did find multiple ways to make sure energy is not wasted:

  • Our lamp is programmed to dim to 50% brightness when no motion is detected. Thus, it still is bright enough to provide light, without wasting too much electricity. If motion is detected, the bulb transitions to full brightness. Once the sensor detects no activity for ten seconds, the bulb dims once again and conserves energy.
  • Further, voice commands work in a similar way. The ESP only listens for voice commands after motion is detected. This way, the device does not need to use up space recording nothing. Additionally, it conserves energy as no power needs to be supplied to the microphone until motion is sensed.
